== Executive Summary

An established European international grocery webshop (15 years on the market,
approximately 1 million daily visitors, 2 million mobile app users) has set a
strategic goal to increase annual turnover by 20%. Today, customer data is
scattered across disconnected systems — the web platform, the mobile app, and
multiple wholesaler feeds are each isolated, making it impossible to understand
customers as individuals or to deliver personalized experiences at scale.

We propose building a **unified customer intelligence platform** that consolidates
all customer data into a single, queryable layer and uses it to power personalized
product suggestions — delivered via email and mobile push notification, every week,
to every customer.

The investment is ~$500K over 7 months. The first campaign goes live at month 5;
full ROI is expected within approximately one year of go-live.

== Business Challenge

The following pressures are limiting revenue growth despite a loyal, growing
customer base:

* **Customer data is siloed.** Web, mobile, and supplier data live in separate
  systems with no unified view of who a customer is, what they buy, or what they
  are likely to buy next.

* **Personalization is not possible at scale.** Every customer receives the same
  marketing communication. Campaigns are broadcast, not targeted — relevant to
  few, ignored by most.

* **The marketing team operates on intuition.** Without consolidated data, campaign
  decisions are based on experience and gut feel rather than evidence. There is no
  feedback loop to measure what works.

* **Revenue growth has plateaued.** With 1 million daily visitors already on the
  platform, the next growth lever is not more traffic — it is a higher value per
  visit. Personalization is the proven mechanism for this.

* **GDPR compliance gaps create legal exposure.** Personal data flows across
  systems without a centralized consent record, audit trail, or automated deletion
  capability — a material regulatory risk.

== Our Recommendation

We recommend building a **unified customer intelligence platform** in three phases
over 7 months.

The platform consolidates customer data from the web shop, the mobile application,
and wholesaler product catalogues into a single, governed data layer. An
intelligence engine analyses purchase history and browsing behaviour to generate
personalized product recommendations for every customer. Recommendations are
delivered weekly via email to start, with the option to move to daily without
rebuilding anything — the platform is designed for it from day one. Real-time
triggers (such as a reminder after a customer browses without purchasing) are a
later capability, once the weekly and daily patterns are established. Mobile push
notifications to the 2 million app users follow in a subsequent phase.

The first personalized email campaign goes live at month 5 — before the platform
is fully complete. This is intentional: it creates an early revenue checkpoint and
validates the recommendation model against real customer behaviour while the
remainder of the platform is still being built.

The platform is designed to be operated by the existing marketing team without a
dedicated data team. Ongoing operation requires no engineering involvement for
day-to-day tasks — the marketing team schedules campaigns, reviews performance
dashboards, and adjusts segments through a self-service interface.

This engagement does not include WordPress migration or re-platforming, new
feature development for the mobile application, EDI or real-time integration with
wholesaler systems, real-time personalisation, or recruitment of a permanent data
team. Any capability beyond this scope is subject to a separate engagement and estimate.

== Our Approach

Four principles guide how this engagement is structured:

. **Value before completion.** The first personalized email campaign goes live at
month 5 — two months before the full platform is complete. This is not a
compromise; it is risk management. Real campaign data validates the recommendation
model early, so the final two months of delivery can incorporate what was learned
rather than launching cold.

. **Built for your team, not ours.** The platform is designed from the ground up
for a non-technical marketing team to operate day-to-day. No specialist is needed
to run weekly campaigns or read performance dashboards. A structured capability
building programme, included in the engagement, transfers knowledge to the internal
team over three months.

. **No disruption to existing operations.** The web shop, mobile application, and
wholesaler relationships are unchanged throughout delivery. The intelligence
platform sits alongside existing systems — it reads from them, but does not replace
or modify them. There is no migration, no downtime, and no risk to current
operations.

. **Privacy by design.** GDPR compliance is built into the data layer from day one —
not added at the end. Customer consent is tracked as a first-class data attribute.
Customers can request deletion of their data and it is actioned within 72 hours,
automatically. A full audit trail of data access is maintained throughout.

== Delivery Milestones

. **Month 1 — Kickoff & Foundation:** Project kickoff, data audit, cloud platform
  provisioned, vendor data access agreements confirmed.

. **Month 3 — Data Pipeline Operational:** All customer data sources connected and
  flowing into the unified data layer. Data quality baseline established. First
  customer profiles built.

. **Month 5 — First Campaign Live** _(revenue checkpoint):_ Personalised email
  campaign sent to the full customer base. Open rates, click rates, and basket size
  uplift measured against control group.

. **Month 7 — Full Platform Go-Live:** Mobile push notification channel active,
  wholesaler product catalogue integrated, recommendation model trained on 3 months
  of real feedback data.

. **Post go-live — Ongoing Operations:** Weekly recommendation refresh, monthly
  performance reviews, quarterly platform review with the internal team. Platform
  continues to improve as more customer interaction data accumulates.

== Key Risks

[cols="3,4", options="header"]
|===
|Risk |Mitigation

|**Customer data quality lower than expected**
|A dedicated data quality audit is the first deliverable of Phase 1. The email
campaign at month 5 is gated on reaching a minimum data quality threshold — the
timeline absorbs a quality remediation sprint without delaying go-live.

|**Vendor coordination delays** (mobile app partner, wholesaler feeds)
|Vendor engagement begins in Week 1 and is tracked as a critical path dependency.
The mobile app vendor has confirmed data access is available on request. Wholesaler
feeds start with the 1–2 most important suppliers; others are added incrementally.

|**No internal team to own the platform long-term**
|A 3-month capability building programme is included in Phase 3 scope. An
operations manual, runbook, and dashboard are delivered at handover. The platform
is explicitly designed to require no engineering involvement for routine operation.
|===
